The iClassifier research tool is designed to provide a data-driven,
macro-level view of classifier systems.

We map the repertoire of classifiers within texts and word lists in different ancient complex scripts (E.g., Ancient Egyptian, Sumerian, ancient Chinese, and Anatolian hieroglyphs), gradually build the semantic range of individual classifiers, and collect the orthographies of lemmas and roots across the corpus.

Our aims ( or "What is yet to be achieved in classifier studies?")

  • (𓏛) Create corpus-based classifier lists.
  • (𓃱) Study the diachrony of a lemma’s classification or of the evolution of a particular classifier.
  • (𓏴) Study synchronous relations between classifier combinations and their inverse co-relations systematically.
  • (𓁐) Uncover distribution patterns by tracking variation in classification according to textual genres, linguistic registers, scribal hands or entities in texts.
  • (𓀀 𒇻 人) Develop a comparative framework for studying classifier systems.
  • (𓁻) Chart language phenomena using network analysis tools.
