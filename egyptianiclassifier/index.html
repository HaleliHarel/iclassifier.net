Using iClassifier

Preliminary access

The pilot version of iClassifier is open to browse and citate in the address: https://iclassifier.pw/reports/

Citation guidelines are specified in this page per report type, and are also generated in each report page.

  • Create corpus-based classifier lists.

    • The tool allows users to generate the list of classifiers in a certain text or group of texts. Existing classifier lists summarize findings in various sources across time and space. The tool presents the repertoire of classifiers of a certain source, time period, script type or geographic area. Users can query and see classifiers by classified Part of Speech and other variables in our dynamic report system:

    For example, the image below shows a list of all classifiers attested with nouns in the different copies of the Ancient Egyptian Tale of Sinuhe.

    Classifier list

    Classifier list of The Tale of Sinuhe, data courtesy of TLA, annotated in iClassifier by Susana Soler.

  • Study the diachrony of a lemma’s classification or the evolution of a particular classifier.

    • Each lexeme or root is presented by showing all its orthographical varities, listing its various classification patterns. For example, here, the classification of the word ḏꜣḏꜣ.t 'assembly; council,' varies across one corpus, The Coffin Texts, and its different classification patterns can are summarized in the lemma form:

    Lemma report

    The Lemma ḏꜣḏꜣ.t 'assembly' in the Coffin Texts, annotated in iClassifier by Jorke Grotenhuis.

    Citation format for Lemma Reports:
    Grotenhuis, Jorke. “ḏꜣḏꜣ.t 'assembly' Lemma Report.” In: iClassifier Reports. Edited by O. Goldwasser, H. Harel, and D. Nikolaev. https://www.iclassifier.pw/reports/CT/lemmas/106, accessed at 15/08/2025.

    In the classifier report, we document the emergance and change of signs in their role of a classifier over time is visualized by tracking its semantic scope and meaning range

    Classifier report

    The classifier 𓂻 (D54) [MOVEMENT] in the Coffin Texts, annotated in iClassifier by Jorke Grotenhuis.

    Grotenhuis, Jorke. “D54 classifier report, Coffin Texts iClassifier.” In: iClassifier Reports. Edited by O. Goldwasser, H. Harel, and D. Nikolaev. https://www.iclassifier.pw/reports/CT/classifiers/D54, accessed at 15/08/2025.
  • Uncover distribution patterns by tracking variation in classification according to textual genres, linguistic registers, scribal hands or entities in texts.

    • Classifier network

    The classifier network of lexical borrowings in Egyptian texts of the New Kingdom, (left): texts in the hieratic script, (right): texts in mounumental hieroglyphic script, annotated in iClassifier by Haleli Harel.

    Citation format for Classifier Networks:
    Harel, Haleli. “Classifying The Other, Classifier Networks.” In: iClassifier Reports. Edited by O. Goldwasser, H. Harel, and D. Nikolaev. https://www.iclassifier.pw/reports/lexicalborrowings/network, accessed at 15/08/2025.

    We study the complex interplay of multiple classifications, by showing the lexical stock and semantic range occuring with a certain classification pattern/class, listing the combination patterns of classifier in each corpus. Users can uncover distribution patterns by tracking variation in classification according to textual genres, linguistic registers, scribal hands or entities in texts. Users can query the project with open queries to find a certain time-space or genre-register query and then compare varieties

  • 𓀀 𒇻 人 Develop a comparative framework for studying classifier systems.

    • We build a typological framework for graphemic classifiers in complex, ancient scripts, and build a toolset to compare classifiers between texts in a script, as well as compare systems using comparative labels assigned to both lemmas and classifiers. This allows us to analyze the semantic range of classifier systems and identify shared and unique classifier categories
